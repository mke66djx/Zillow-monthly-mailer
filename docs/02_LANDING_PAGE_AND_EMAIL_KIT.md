### Email 2: Day 2

Subject:

Why permits matter more than people think

Body:

Rent and value trends tell you what already moved. Permits can tell you what might be changing underneath the surface.

New units can create future supply pressure. Renovations, ADUs, electrical upgrades, roofing, and remodels can suggest reinvestment. Contractor activity can show where builders and owners are placing money.

The report does not treat all permits the same. A water heater is not the same as a new duplex. A roofing cluster is not the same as a multifamily project. The goal is to separate noise from useful signal.

### Email 3: Day 5

Subject:

What makes a ZIP interesting?

Body:

The best ZIPs are rarely the ones with one huge number.

I am looking for combinations:

- Rent growth ahead of value growth.
- Reasonable gross yield.
- Enough population and history to trust the trend.
- Moderate or low new supply pressure.
- Rising renovation or ADU activity.
- Evidence that the signal is not just a weird data spike.

That is the difference between a chart and a useful investor radar.
