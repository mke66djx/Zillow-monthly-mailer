# Sacramento Investor Radar: Executable Launch Plan

## Product Decision

Build `Sacramento Investor Radar` as a monthly investor intelligence product, starting with an email list and a polished monthly brief. The dashboard becomes the paid research room after the brief proves demand.

Positioning:

> Monthly ZIP, rent, permit, and contractor intelligence for Sacramento real estate investors.

The product should not compete head-on with property-listing, underwriting, or raw-data platforms. It should own the investor workflow before a deal search starts:

1. Where should I focus this month?
2. Why is that area interesting?
3. What evidence supports the signal?
4. What could prove it wrong?
5. Who is building, renovating, or pulling permits there?

## Why This Can Win

Competitors already cover broad pieces:

- Reventure and PropertyIQ cover macro ZIP/county/metro housing data and scoring.
- Mashvisor covers rental investment analytics, STR/LTR comparisons, and heat maps.
- Privy and PropStream focus on active property/deal discovery and lead generation.
- DealCheck focuses on underwriting individual properties.
- Rentometer and AirDNA focus on rent or STR comp intelligence.
- BuildZoom owns broad permit and contractor data.
- ATTOM, HouseCanary, and NeighborhoodScout are deeper enterprise or broad-data providers.

The gap is a curated local investor brief that blends rent/value dislocation, local permit signals, contractor activity, and confidence warnings into an actionable thesis.

## Initial Stack

Recommended email platform: Beehiiv first.

Reasons:

- Free launch plan supports up to 2,500 subscribers, custom domains, analytics, unlimited sends, and a recommendation network.
- Scale plan adds automations, surveys, paid subscriptions, and 0% take rate on paid subscriptions.
- It is built for newsletter growth, which matters more than complex CRM during the first 90 days.

Alternatives:

- Substack if speed and built-in paid newsletter infrastructure matter more than brand control. Substack is free to start but takes 10% of paid subscription revenue.
- Kit if creator automations and tagging matter more than publication-style growth.
- Buttondown if we want a clean developer-friendly newsletter tool later.

## Revenue Model

Start free. Do not charge until the report has 4 consecutive issues and at least 100 local subscribers.

Phase pricing:

- Free: public monthly teaser with top 3 ZIPs and one permit/contractor highlight.
- Paid Brief: $19/month or $149/year for full monthly report, top 10 ZIPs, warnings, and CSV attachments.
- Pro Research Room: $49/month or $399/year for dashboard access, ZIP thesis pages, contractor drilldowns, and archive.
- Team/Agent: $99/month for branded exports, watchlists, and client-ready PDF reports.

## Success Metrics

First 30 days:

- 100 email subscribers.
- 20 direct investor conversations.
- 10 replies to the welcome email.
- 5 people who ask for the full report or dashboard.

First 90 days:

- 500 subscribers.
- 50 monthly active readers.
- 10 paid customers.
- 3 referral partners: investor-friendly agent, lender, property manager, contractor.

Do not optimize for social followers first. Optimize for email subscribers and replies.

## 30-Day Execution Plan

### Week 1: Foundation

- Pick name and domain.
- Create Beehiiv publication.
- Publish landing page.
- Generate first sample report from the current data pipeline.
- Create one clean sample chart set:
  - Top ZIP opportunities.
  - Rent/value gap.
  - New supply pressure.
  - Renovation pulse.
  - Contractor leaderboard.
- Add legal/disclaimer footer.
- Add tracking sheet for leads, replies, and conversations.

### Week 2: Launch The Free Teaser

- Publish first free Sacramento Investor Radar issue.
- Send to personal contacts who are investors, agents, lenders, contractors, or property managers.
- Post 10 high-signal snippets on LinkedIn/X.
- Ask every reader one question: "What ZIPs or contractor activity do you want watched next month?"
- Add replies to a feedback log.

### Week 3: Build The Paid Brief

- Create the paid issue format.
- Add the full ZIP top 10 table.
- Add the full contractor radar.
- Add the supply warning section.
- Add the low-confidence warning section.
- Add CSV attachment links or download links.
- Recruit 5 beta readers manually.

### Week 4: Close Beta Users

- Offer founding subscriber pricing.
- Run 10 calls with local investors/agents.
- Ask what would make the brief worth $19/month.
- Ship one improved issue based on feedback.
- Decide whether dashboard access is needed immediately or should wait.

## California Expansion Path

Expand only after Sacramento has repeated usage.

Expansion criteria:

- Public permit feed exists and can be pulled reliably.
- Zillow ZIP/county/metro data has enough coverage.
- Apartment List or another rent source has city/county support.
- Market has enough investor demand.
- There is a clear local angle: ADUs, infill, new supply, rent control, major employers, migration, insurance risk, or zoning change.

Likely order:

1. Sacramento region.
2. San Joaquin / Stockton.
3. Placer / Roseville / Rocklin.
4. Yolo / Woodland / Davis.
5. Bay Area investor spillover markets.
6. Fresno / Central Valley.
7. Inland Empire.
8. San Diego or Los Angeles only after we have stronger local-data normalization.

## Operating Cadence

Weekly:

- One public chart.
- One contractor/permit highlight.
- One ZIP thesis post.
- One email list ask.

Monthly:

- Full Zillow refresh.
- Permit refresh.
- Sacramento Investor Radar issue.
- Archive report.
- Update leaderboard.
- Review top false positives.

Quarterly:

- Revisit scoring weights.
- Compare against real-world rent/listing/property outcomes.
- Add one new California market if Sacramento workflow is stable.
